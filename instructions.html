Assembly Instructions

Follow these steps to assemble your 3D-printed prosthetic hand with servo-actuated finger control.

Step 1 — Print and Prepare Components

Begin by 3D printing all required parts using PLA or ABS filament. You will need:

  • Hand palm assembly
  • 5 finger segments (thumb, index, middle, ring, pinky)
  • Forearm mount
  • Servo motor housing

Clean up any support material and test-fit all joints to ensure smooth articulation.

Step 2 — Thread the Control Wires

Cut fishing line or nylon thread to appropriate lengths for each finger (approximately 30-40cm). Thread the wire through the finger segments:

  1. Insert wire through the tip of each finger
  2. Route through the knuckle joints
  3. Pull wire down through the palm assembly
  4. Leave 10-15cm of excess wire at the base for servo attachment

Tip: Use a thin needle or wire threader to guide the wire through narrow channels.

Step 3 — Install Servo Motors

Mount the servo motors in the forearm housing or base assembly:

  1. Place 5 servo motors (one per finger) in their designated slots
  2. Secure each servo with screws or mounting brackets
  3. Attach servo horns and prepare for wire connection
  4. Connect the control wires from each finger to its corresponding servo horn
  5. Test tension — pulling the wire should curl the finger, releasing should extend it

Note: Add elastic return springs to the back of fingers if needed for passive extension.

Step 4 — Wire the Electronics

Connect the servo motors to your microcontroller (Arduino, Raspberry Pi, etc.):

  • Power: Connect all servo power wires (red) to 5-6V power supply
  • Ground: Connect all ground wires (brown/black) to common ground
  • Signal: Connect each servo signal wire (orange/yellow) to individual PWM pins on the microcontroller
  • Use a breadboard or custom PCB for organized wiring

Important: Ensure power supply can handle the current draw of all 5 servos simultaneously (typically 1-2A).

Step 5 — Program the Controller

Upload control code to your microcontroller to enable finger movement:

  1. Install the Arduino IDE or Python libraries for your platform
  2. Import servo control libraries (e.g., Servo.h for Arduino)
  3. Follow our Programming Tutorial to set up basic finger control
  4. Calibrate servo positions for fully open and closed finger states
  5. Test individual finger movements before proceeding

Sample Code: Basic servo control examples are available in the tutorial section.

Step 6 — Test and Calibrate

With everything assembled and programmed:

  • Test each finger individually to verify proper range of motion
  • Adjust wire tension if fingers don't fully close or open
  • Check for binding or excessive friction in joints
  • Program grip patterns (pinch, grasp, point, etc.)
  • Add sensor input if using EMG or other control methods

Safety: Always test at low speeds first to avoid damaging the mechanism.

⚠️ Safety & Maintenance

  • Never exceed servo voltage ratings (typically 4.8-6V)
  • Regularly inspect wires for fraying or wear
  • Lubricate joints with silicone grease if movement becomes stiff
  • Keep electronics away from water and moisture
